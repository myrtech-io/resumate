What does a Good resume mean?

The resume acts as a bridge between you and the prospective recruiter. So, to make the first impression, it is imperative that your resume stands out from the crowd. A well-written resume does half of the work for you. Hence it is important to have a structured and concise resume to make the first impression. cvDragon won’t let your efforts go in vain, we provide a platform where you can get what you want in a resume. A good resume has some important features :

  • A good first impression :

    You must have heard of the saying that the first impression is the last impression. It goes well with resumes as well. In the sea of mediocrity, if one resume stands out and it gives a feeling that yes, this is the one, then working hard on your resume is worth it. The recruiter would likely ignore the resume which is text-heavy and difficult to read. Try to keep it simple, easy to read, and easy to understand.

  • A good clarification to convince others :

    Convincing people is the most important part. Convincing the recruiter why you and why not the other person must be done by giving some clarity in the recruiter’s mind. And that clarification is given by your resume that you are the most eligible candidate for the position.

  • Devoid of jargons and buzzwords :

    Do not make it or full of jargon to make it cumbersome for a recruiter to read. Another thing you need to keep in mind is that when you are sending your resume to a recruiter, send the resume only or at the most attach a cover letter. Also do not include or attach a letter of recommendation, copies of your education mark sheets, reference letter, or anything else unless this is asked for.

What does a Bad resume mean?

A really awful resume is something no employer wants. They can’t do anything with it, so the fruitcake and resume both get tossed and end up sitting in the garbage together, getting to know each other. Your lack of preparation in getting a resume done should not come off like an afterthought at the 11th hour. Plan ahead, do it right, and you won’t regret it. A bad resume consists of:

  • Typos and meaningless words :

    Plan ahead, do it right, and you won’t regret it. If you’re rewriting your resume and customizing it for each position you apply for which you should be doing, make sure your rewrites and formatting don’t add mistakes or omissions. Hiring managers and human resources professionals don’t want to see these terms on your resume, including best of the breed, go-getter, think outside of the box, synergy, go-to person, thought leadership,detail-oriented, proactively and track record. Skip these buzzwords and instead include specific accomplishments and results to prove value added to hiring managers.

  • Unnecessary information :

    “I want this job” isn’t adding anything new to your resume, and instead only serves to take up valuable resume real estate. Employers will come away more informed if you include summaries of your qualifications, accomplishments, and skills.

  • Bad formatting :

    It is important to ensure that you’re using the right type of document when creating and sending your resume, but you also need to make sure that your resume gets delivered in the correct formatting, too. We at cvDragon ensures that we use the simple formats where it is easy to convert your resume in pdf or Docx, depends on you in which format you want to take.
